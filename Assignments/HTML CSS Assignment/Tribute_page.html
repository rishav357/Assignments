One of the most vibrant chess prodigy of all time

Robert James Bobby Fischer was an American chess grandmaster and the eleventh World Chess Champion.
He was the first ever World Champion to have crossed 2700+ ELO rating.

Achievements of Bobby fischer :

  • 1943 Robert James Fischer was born in Chicago on March 9, Illinois.
    Fischer's parents divorced when he was a toddler, and he began learning chess at the age of 6 after his older sister Joan bought him a chess set.
    He continued to hone his skills as a youngster at the Brooklyn Chess Club and Manhattan Chess Club.
  • 1957 A brilliant, highly competitive player who lost himself in the game, Fischer earned a place in the record books at age 14 when he became the youngest player to win the U.S. Chess Championship.
  • 1958 in 1958, at 15, he became the youngest international grandmaster in history by placing sixth at a tournament in Portoroz, Yugoslavia (now Slovenia).
  • 1960 During the early 1960s, Fischer continued to be involved in U.S. and world championship matches but was also making a name for himself with his erratic, paranoid commentary.
  • 1970 After having a 20-game winning streak in the early 1970s, Fischer once again made chess history in 1972 with his defeat of the Soviet Union's Boris Spassky at the Reykjavik, Iceland world championships, thus marking the first time an American chess player had won the title.
